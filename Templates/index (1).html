Stock Correlation App

Computes correlated stocks based on historical prices. If stock alpha has daily prices A[0], A[1], A[2], ..., A[N-1], and stock beta has daily prices B[0], B[1], B[2], ..., B[N-1], then their correlation is the number of days they move in the same direction minus the number of days they move in opposite directions. (Formulaically, it's (N-1) - |{i, 0 < 1 < N-1 | sgn(A[i+1] - A[i]) != sgn(B[i+1] - B[i])}|.)

Text Entry

Enter the stocks here in this comma-seperated format:
xom,12,14,16
ge,10,20,30
ibm,100,100,100
(The first entry is the stock symbol, the following are the prices each day.)
